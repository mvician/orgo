RESEARCH

beta blockers

what is it?

Beta blockers are a wide variety of medications that lower blood pressure.

Beta blockers tend to be used to treat problems such as arrhythmia, heart failure and heart attacks.

On top of this, beta blockers may prevent future heart problems in patients with heart disease.

function

Beta blockers function as antagonists by inhibiting epinephrine (or adrenaline) from binding to beta-adrenoreceptors such as B1 (found in the heart) and B2 (found in smooth muscle). This causes the heart to pump slower as well as pump at a lower force. On top of this, the inhibition also has the effect of widening arteries and veins.

Propranolol is a non-selective antagonist. Non-selective beta blockers tend to have an equal affinity for both B1 and B2 receptors.

As shown in the figure, propranolol exhibits an oxy propanolamine (defining feature of general beta blockers) as well as an aromatic moiety. It is believed that the aromatic moiety binds to the lipid bilayer while the oxy propanolamine remains in the hydrophilic region. Some research indicates that the amine group may interact electrostatically with the phosphate groups of the lipid bilayer.

fun fact

Propranolol, like many other beta blockers, is sold as a racemic mixture where the contents
are made up of S as well as R enantiomers. Interestingly, only the S enantiomer exhibits affinity
for the inhibition of beta-adrenoreceptors.

On the other hand, only the R enantiomer is capable of inhibiting the conversion
of thyroxine to triiodothyronine (a process that influences heart rate).

As a result, the way in which the R and S enantiomers can interact with other
chiral compounds differ and, therefore, complement each other.
